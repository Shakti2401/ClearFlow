What is Clearing?

Clearing is the exchange of payment instructions between banks before money actually settles. Your bank and the receiving bank have to verify, confirm, and reconcile the instruction — cheque, NEFT, or RTGS — before funds move for real.

This simulator walks one instrument through that entire lifecycle, stage by stage.

Received→ Verification→ Clearing House→ Settlement→ Completed

↗ Outward Clearing

A customer deposits a cheque drawn on another bank. Your bank is the collecting bank — it sends the instrument outward, through the clearing house, to the bank it's drawn on.

Your Bank (collecting) → Clearing House → Drawee Bank

↙ Inward Clearing

Another bank presents a cheque drawn on your bank. Your bank is the paying bank — it must verify the account and signature, then honour or reject it.

Presenting Bank → Clearing House → Your Bank (paying)
